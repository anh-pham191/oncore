    To meet the Healthy Home Standards, existing ceiling insulation must be at least 120mm thick, be in reasonable condition (no dampness, mould, or gaps), and meet the 2008 Building Code minimum r-value for your zone. Also, underfloor insulation must meet a minimum r-value of 1.3.

    WHO DO THESE STANDARDS IMPACT?

    The Healthy Home Standards affect landlords and their tenants. Landlords are required by law to meet the minimum housing requirements set out in the Healthy Home Standards. If a landlord is non-compliant, a tenant can apply to the Tenancy Tribunal for a monetary order and/or a work order. The Tribunal may award a financial penalty of up to $4,000 against the landlord, which is usually paid to the tenant.

    WHEN DO PROPERTY FIXES NEED TO BE COMPLETED?

    From 1st July 2021, landlords must ensure that all rental homes comply with the Standards within 90 days of any new or renewed tenancy. By 1st July 2024, all rental homes must comply with the Standards, regardless of when the tenancy began.

    COMPLIANCE TIMEFRAMES

    1st July 2020

    New, varied, and renewed tenancy agreements must include specific information regarding the property's current level of Healthy Homes compliance.

    Note that this is not required if the tenancy is a fixed term agreement that ends prior to 1st July 2021.
    1st July 2021

    Landlords must ensure that all rental homes comply with the Standards within 90 days of any new or renewed tenancy.

    1st July 2023

    All Kainga Ora Homes and houses rented out by Housing New Zealand and Community Housing Providers must meet the Standards.

    1st July 2024

    All rental homes must comply with the Standards regardless of when the tenancy began.
