To meet these standards, you must meet the regulations set out below. Guides and tools will be available online from July 1st 2019 onward to assist landlords in understanding their obligations.

Who do these standards impact?

The healthy homes standards affect landlords and their tenants. Landlords will be required by law to meet the minimum housing requirements set out in the Healthy Homes Standards.

When do the property fixes need to be in place by?

Landlords will be required to meet the standards by 1st July, 2021. This excludes tenancies under Housing New Zealand and registered community housing providers, who must meet the standards by 1st July 2023.

Learn more about the requirements to meet the standards

Heating

To meet the Healthy Homes’ heating standards, you must have a fixed device in your home’s main living area that reaches at least 18 degrees Celsius.

Certain heating devices will not meet requirements. This includes open fires, unflued combustion heaters and electric heaters (excluding heat pumps). If your home’s living area currently doesn’t meet these standards, Oncore can advise you on the best product for your home and will provide a high-quality installation.

Insulation

Your rental property’s ceiling and underfloor insulation must be up to the standards of the 2008 New Zealand Building Code, and ceiling insulation needs to be at least 120mm in thickness.

Additionally, your insulation needs to meet the Healthy Homes Standards’ definition of being in “reasonable condition”. If you’re not sure if your insulation currently meets these standards, Oncore can help. We’ll inspect your insulation and upgrade it as needed.

Ventilation

When it comes to meeting the Healthy Homes’ ventilation standards, there are a few things to check for.

Firstly, there must be windows which can open to the equivalent of 5% of the floor area in your property’s bedrooms, dining rooms, living rooms, lounges, open plan rooms and kitchens. Secondly, bathrooms and kitchens (or rooms containing a bath, shower or cooktop) must contain a sufficiently sized extractor fan which provides external ventilation. If you’re unsure of whether or not your extractor fan meets these standards, or if you require window installations, get in touch with Oncore to arrange a consultation.

Moisture ingress and drainage

The moisture ingress and drainage standards cover efficient drainage, roof water removal and ground moisture barriers.

Firstly, you will be required to ensure there are sufficient drainage systems in place at your rental property, which will remove surface, storm and groundwater. Secondly, efficient gutters, downpipes and drains must be installed. Thirdly, if you have an enclosed subfloor cavity, it must be with a ground moisture barrier that meets New Zealand standards. Oncore can provide you with a moisture ingress and drainage inspection and take the necessary steps to prevent any future water damage to your property.

Draught Stopping

The main goals behind the draught stopping standard are to ensure your tenants stay warm and comfortable year round, in an energy efficient manner. Keep a lookout for any significant gaps under doors, around windows, within the walls or in the floors.

If your property includes an unused fireplace or chimney, these will need to be blocked in order to reduce any unnecessary draughts. If any areas of your property require draught stopping, Oncore can make the necessary repairs; ensuring your property is healthy, comfortable and meets national regulations.
